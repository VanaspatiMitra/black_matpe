About the Crop:

Black Matpe - T9 is a high-yielding variety of black gram (Vigna mungo), notable for its resilience and adaptability. It thrives in well-drained, sandy loam or loamy soils with a pH range of 6.0 to 7.0. For optimal growth, it requires about 600-800 mm of annual rainfall or equivalent irrigation. On average, Black Matpe - T9 can produce 800-1,000 kg of pulses per acre. The profit margins can be quite favorable, often varying between 20-30% depending on market prices and input costs.

  • Soil Composition: well-drained loamy to clay loam soils with a pH range of 6.0 to 7.0c
  • Water need per Acre: 1.97 to 2.62 feet
  • Potential Dangers: Soil Degradation, Pest Infestation, Waterlogging
  • Fertilizers: Diammonium Phosphate, Organic Matter, Potassium, Nitrogen
  • Pesticides: Emamectin benzoate ,Thiamethoxam, Acetamiprid
